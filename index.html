  • Overview of the Research Progress Review Process

  • Q1. What is the purpose of the Research Progress Review?

    The purpose of the Research Progress Review is to assess the progress of higher degree research (HDR) candidates and ensure they are on track to complete their degree in a timely manner. It provides an opportunity to:

    • Affirm the candidate's progress if satisfactory.
    • Develop a research plan and set new milestones for the next review period.
    • Review the research and supervision arrangements with an independent panel.
    • Offer a safe environment for the candidate to raise any issues impacting their progress.
  • Q2. What types of reviews exist?

    The types of reviews in the UNSW Research Progress Review process are:

    • Annual Research Progress Review: Conducted yearly to assess the candidate's progress and ensure they are on track.
    • Confirmation Review: Required for PhD candidates within the first year (FTE) to confirm they have a feasible research plan and have met all necessary milestones.
    • Overtime Review: For candidates who exceed the maximum time for their degree. Progress is reviewed every 3 months to ensure the research is nearing completion.
  • Q3. What is Confirmation of Candidature?

    Confirmation of Candidature is a formal process required for all PhD candidates, typically scheduled around 0.75 FTE from the start date of the term in which the candidate commenced, and no later than 1 year FTE. EFTSL. The purpose is to confirm that:

    • The candidate has developed a feasible and detailed research program suitable for a doctoral degree.
    • The candidate has demonstrated the capacity to undertake research at the doctoral level.
    • All mandatory milestones, such as a literature review, research proposal, and any required training, have been completed.

    Successful confirmation is essential for the candidate to continue their PhD program. Where confirmation of candidature cannot be completed within 1.25 FTE, the candidate may be asked to Show Cause.

  • Q4. What is Overtime Review?

    Candidates must apply for overtime enrolment when candidates in their final term of on-time candidature may not be on track to submit on time. Overtime enrolment request must follow the guidline in Section 9 of Variation of Candidature Procedure.

    An overtime review is conducted when a research candidate exceeds the maximum allowable time for their degree. During the overtime period, candidates' progress is reviewed at least every 3 months. Candidates can apply for up to one year of overtime enrolment. See Section 9 of Research Progress Review and Confirmation of Research Candidatures Procedure.

  • Q5. What is the frequency of APR?

    All full-time and part-time HDR candidates must have their research progress reviewed by an independent panel at least once per calendar year of enrolment. Re-enrolment in each year of an HDR program up to the maximum time for the degree is conditional upon undergoing a Review within the year. For part-time candidates, progress is evaluated based on full-time equivalent (FTE) time periods. See Section 4 of Research Progress Review and Confirmation of Research Candidatures Procedure.

  • Q6. When should I do ARP after a program leave?

    If a candidate is going on approved program leave, a Review should be held immediately before the leave or, if not possible, within three months of returning to study, but no earlier than one month after their return. See Section 4.3 of Research Progress Review and Confirmation of Research Candidatures Procedure.

  • Q6. How is the progress of part-time candidates evaluated？

    While all candidates must be reviewed once per calendar year, progress is evaluated based on full-time equivalent (FTE) time periods for part-time candidates. In cases where part-time candidates have received a marginal or unsatisfactory outcome, the follow-up Review would be held within 6 months (i.e., 3 months FTE).

  • How to Prepare Your review

  • Q7. What is the process leading up to the review?

    All full-time and part-time HDR candidates must have their research progress reviewed by an independent panel at least once per calendar year of enrolment

    • You will receive an email about the upcoming review.
    • You and your supervisor will need to complete the form on GRIS
    • For the first review, the school will arrange at least two panel members for you.
    • The school will schedule a review meeting.
    • You must attend the review on time. A candidate will be asked to Show Cause if they do not attend a scheduled research progress review.
  • Q8. Is the oral presentation necessary?

    An oral presentation on the research must be given. This can be a presentation directly to the panel or it could be an open presentation to the School.

  • Q9. What documents do I need to prepare?

    Ideally, you should complete your section of the review form on GRIS 14 days prior to the scheduled review date or by the deadline specified in the email.

    You can check the required documents that need to be completed via HDR Progress Review - Candidate Checklist

  • Q10. What is the role of milestones?

    The role of milestones in the research process is to:

    • Help manage progress by breaking down the research plan into short, medium, and long-term goals.
    • Provide a clear framework for tracking progress and identifying areas that need attention.
    • Allow both the candidate and supervisors to regularly assess how much progress has been made and adjust timelines or strategies if needed.
    • Ensure that the candidate stays on track for timely completion of their research.

    Milestones are reviewed and updated at each Research Progress Review to reflect the candidate’s progress and set achievable goals for the next review period.

  • Q10. When should the initial candidate milestone be decided?

    Supervisors must discuss milestones for the initial Progress Review period with candidates in the first three months of enrolment.

  • In the Review

  • Q11. What is the review process?

    During a review, the following occurs:

    • The candidate presents their research progress to an independent panel.
    • The panel assesses the candidate's progress against set milestones and provides feedback.
    • There are discussions about research plans, supervision arrangements, and any issues affecting progress.
    • The review outcome is determined, and new milestones are set for the next review period.
    • The candidate has the opportunity to raise any concerns in a confidential meeting with the panel, without the supervisor present.

    The review session is run in two parts. As a candidate under review, you are required to attend the whole session as follows:

    Presentation
    Oral presentation by candidate : 20 minutes
    Questions : 5+ minutes
    TotalPresentation Time : 30 minutes approx
    Review Meeting
    Joint panel discussion with candidate & supervisor(s) : 5-10 minutes
    Panel discussion with supervisor(s) : 5 minutes
    Panel discussion with candidate : 5 minutes
    Wrap-up : 5 minutes
    Total Review Time : 30 minutes approx.
  • Q12. What are the possible review outcomes?

    The possible outcomes of a review are:

    • Satisfactory: The candidate has met all milestones and is on track to complete their research on time. New milestones are set for the next review.
    • Marginal: Some milestones were not fully met, but the candidate is given the opportunity to address these issues. A follow-up review is scheduled within 3 months (FTE).
    • Unsatisfactory: Progress is significantly below expectations. The candidate is given 3 months (FTE) to meet the milestones, and failure to do so may result in a Show Cause process.

  • After the Review

  • Q13. Appeals and Complaints

    Candidates can appeal on the basis of procedural fairness if they believe that procedures were not properly followed or if there is a conflict of interest. Appeals must be submitted to the Faculty Higher Degree Committee. For more details, refer to Section 11 of Research Progress Review Procedure.

  • Q13. What should I do if I get Satisfactory?

    Set a new milestone for the next review.

  • Q13. What should I do if I get Marginal or Unsatisfactory?

    It may indicate that the candidate is not suited to undertaking a Doctoral program. For the candidature to continue, the following actions must be taken:

    • Set new milestones.
    • You will need to do a second review in 3 months. You may receive a Show Cause if you fail to pass the second review.
    • The Confirmation period cannot be extended beyond 15 months (FTE) from initial enrolment.

    See Section 8.2 and 8.3 of Research Progress Review and Confirmation of Research Candidatures Procedure

  • Q13. What is the requirement of overtime enrolment?
    • Overtime enrolment cannot be considered unless a Progress Review has been held in the final term of enrolment.
    • Any final on-time reviews (recent review before overtime enrolment) can only be marginal or unsatisfactory.
    • Any overtime enrolment should be applied before the candidature lapses.
    • Candidates may request up to one year FTE of Overtime Enrolment.
    • If the candidate chooses to apply for less than a one-year FTE period in their first overtime request, and subsequently requires more time, they will need to apply for further overtime enrolment through the HDC.
    • Have a detailed and realistic timeline for completion which will inform the setting of feasible milestones.

    See Section 9.1 of Research Progress Review and Confirmation of Research Candidatures Procedure

  • Q14. Whom should you contact when you need help?

    You can find the contact details of the Postgraduate Research Coordinator and the Head of School on the CSE contact page.

  • Show Cause

  • Q15. What is Show Cause?

    When you receive a Show Cause, the university believes you will not be possible to complete the degree under the current circumstances. Show Cause provides an opportunity for the candidate to demonstrate to the University that they have a detailed, feasible and supported plan to complete their research degree.

  • Q16. What are the reasons for receiving a Show Cause?

    Following cases will lead to Show Cause:

    • You did not attend the scheduled review.
    • You received two consecutive unsatisfactory results from the review.
    • You received an unsatisfactory result from the overtime review.
    • Lapsed candidature.

    See Section 2 of the Show Cause Guideline.

  • Q16. What is lapsed candidature?

    Where a candidate has not submitted their thesis nor submitted an overtime enrolment request by the term end date of the final term of enrolment, their candidature will be considered “lapsed”.

  • Q17. What are the consequences after Show Cause?

    The University will make a decision on whether the candidate should be discontinued, or whether the candidate should be given an opportunity to complete their candidature under strict conditions.

  • Q18. What steps do I need to follow when I receive a Show Cause?

    Candidates are requested to Show Cause in a letter from the Dean of Graduate Research. The candidate is asked to provide a formal written response which is assessed by the relevant Faculty Higher Degree Committee (the ‘Committee’). The Committee makes a recommendation to the Dean of Graduate Research as to whether the candidature should be discontinued, or whether the candidate should be given an opportunity to complete their candidature under strict conditions. The Dean of Graduate Research makes the final decision regarding the outcome of the case. Full details refer to Section 5 of Show Cause Guideline.

  • Variation FAQ

  • Q19. How many days for standard sick leave and annual leave for a HDR candidate

    You are entitled to 20 working days of annual leave each year. This type of leave doesn't accrue over multiple years, so you need to take it each year of your candidature. You are entitled to up to 10 working days standard sick leave each year of your scholarship. You can use this leave to take time off if you are unwell, or if you have family caring responsibilities.

  • Q20. what if I could like an extended sick leave (more than 10 working days)?

    If you need to take leave for more than 10 works days because you are unwell or have family caring responsibilities, you can access up to 60 working days of extended sick leave in total during your candidature. You must apply following the Paid Scholarship Leave page.

  • Supervisor FAQ

  • Q21. What do supervisors need to do for APR?

    Supervisors must discuss milestones for the initial Progress Review period with candidates in the first three months of enrolment. Supervisors should complete the relevant parts of the review form on GRIS before the review. Supervisors are encouraged to attend the review

  • Pannel FAQ

  • Q22. How many members should be in the panel?

    At least two.

  • Q22. What are the requirements for panel composition?
    • A panel chair.
    • At least one other member who holds an appointment at UNSW academic level B or higher.
    • There is at most one member who is an early career researcher.
    • There is at most one member who is external to UNSW.
    • The same panel members should be maintained for Reviews of a candidate throughout their candidature wherever possible.

    See Section 5 of Research Progress Review and Confirmation of Research Candidatures Procedure for more details.

  • Q22. Setting Milestones for Future Reviews.

    In all Reviews, regardless of the outcome, the panel, together with the candidate and the supervisor(s), must set milestones for the next Review, and these will be used to assess progress in the program. Section 6.3 of Research Progress Review and Confirmation of Research Candidatures Procedure.

  • Q22. Managing issues relating to supervision.

    If the panel is alerted to a potential dispute or breakdown in the relationship between the candidate and the supervisor, both the candidate and the supervisor should be given the opportunity to meet with the panel separately to discuss the relevant issues. The panel should discuss any concerns with the School PGC.
